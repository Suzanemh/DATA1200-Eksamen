Christmas markets

From mid-November and through December, several Christmas markets are open in Oslo. Some are day or weekend events, while the perhaps larges one in town, located in Karl Johan runs for almost two months straight. They are a good place to browse for that special Christmas gift, or just get a hot beverage and a snack.

Walk along Akerselva

The River Akerselva runs through five districts in Oslo, and is 9.8 kilometors long. Walking along it, either from start to finish or parts of it, is a popular route for a hike. Along it one can find nice places to have a full meal, or pick up a pastery and coffee. Winter and summer, it's a great walk.

Deichman Library in Bjørvika

The new Deichman library in Bjørvika is a lovely place to sit, both to study and also to relax. It's become one of my favourite places read a good book on a rainy day. If you have childre, they also have occasional loud readings of childlrens books.

Take a dip at Sørenga

Sørenga is the perfect place to take a quick dip in the water. Take a dip, grab an ice-cream and relax with the people of Oslo on the docks.

The Oslofjord Islands

The Oslofjord has a handful of small islands that can be accessed with your regular public transportation ticket. These are the perfect destination for getting away from the hustle and bustle of the city. The islands are green, gorgeous, and very relaxing. Perfect for the day-excurtion.
