What can you do as an individual to help tackle the problem of mis- and disinformation on the internet, both in terms of your personal and your professional life?

On the internet, anyone can share information, and therefore, as an individual, we should be more skeptical of the information we read by being source critical (LINK). Not believing everything you read is the first step to reducing this problem. Many articles have clickbait titles that are misleading (LINK) with the intention to make people curious and in that way earn money. The title often gives false information (LINK), while the rest of the texts give the correct information. Reading the whole text is therefore important. Given that everybody with an internet connection can potentially spread fake news, one of the important things you as an individual can do to prevent spread of mis- and disinformation, is to always be critical of the information you read and hear yourself, so you don’t contribute to the spread. Even though the information seems legit, you should always check several sources before spreading it further yourself. If you’re doubtful, check with a site like Snopes.com or factcheck.org (Kiely, E. 2016). One thing you can actively do is to call out people in for example comment sections that are spreading fake news. A lot of these people are just saying things to provoke and start a fight, so you calling them out is maybe what they actually want to happen. In these cases, it's better to just report them so that a moderator can remove their comment and possibly block them from commenting bullshit on things in the future. Even though newspapers should be reliable sources, they are not always. They can spread fake news because somebody has set them up to fool them, or they can have posts from opinionated people that are not supposed to be factual (leserinnlegg), but not everybody will understand that this is not pure facts.
